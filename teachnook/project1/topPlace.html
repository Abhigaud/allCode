Sanchi Stupa, Madhya Pradesh

Sanchi Stupa is one of the oldest stone structures in India, built by Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Taj Mahal, Agra

The Taj Mahal is one of the most famous monuments in the world. Built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, it is a symbol of love.

Mysore Palace, Karnataka

Mysore Palace is an iconic landmark known for its Indo-Saracenic architecture and grand Dussehra celebrations.

Gateway of India, Mumbai

Gateway of India, built in 1924, is a historic arch-monument in Mumbai, symbolizing India's colonial history and independence.

Sun Temple, Konark

The Sun Temple in Konark, built in the 13th century, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for its intricate stone carvings.

Jaisalmer Fort, Rajasthan

Jaisalmer Fort, also known as Sonar Quila, is one of the largest forts in the world, built in 1156 AD.
